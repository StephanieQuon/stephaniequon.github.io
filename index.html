Technical Experience

Research Assistant

Neuroscience, Engagement, and Smart Technology Lab | September 2020 - Present

I am a research assistant at the NEST (Neuroscience, Engagement, and Smart Technology) lab of Neuroethics Canada! My work consists of programming and designing interactive robotics demonstrations (Python, C), and leading a study focused on stigma and emotional alignment in the use of social robots with older adults.

Undergraduate Teaching Assistant

UBC Department of Computer Science | September 2020 - Present

I am a TA for APSC 160 Computation in Engineering, an introductory programming class on C. In this role, I help answer questions from students during lecture, help students debug their code, and mark exams.

Software Developer

Digital Health Innovation Lab, BC Children's Hospital Research | May 2020 - Present

For the summer of 2020, I received an NSERC Undergraduate Student Research Award from UBC. I worked on a project titled "Smartphone Video-Based Respiratory Rate Tracking for Infants". Leading this project, I utilized computer vision and signal processing tools (MATLAB) to create an algorithm that stabilizes videos, identifies tracking stickers, tracks movement, and estimates respiratory rate from video. I am now working as a developer for cross-platform app with synchronous recording and haptic feedback features, for use in a global health study in Lilongwe, Malawi (Java, C++, Scheme, LambdaNative).

Signal Processing and App Development Team Member

UBC Biomedical Engineering Student Team | September 2019 - Present

I am part of UBC BEST's neurotech team: MINT! This team works to build a working electroencephalogram (EEG) for the fixed and open challenges of the NeuroTechX competition. On the team, I am responsible for analyzing EEG signals (MATLAB), and developing the login/database of an Android app (Java, Android Studio).

Founder & President

The Sprouts Initiative | August 2019 - Present

In August 2017, I started The Sprouts Initiative, an organization focused on 3 pillars: Accessibility, Sustainability, and Wellbeing. The organization has received over $40,000 in grants and involved 100+ youth in leadership and programs, with recognition from The Globe and Mail, Bloomberg, The Canadian Wildlife Federation, and more. Some of our successful projects include distributing thousands of reusable straws across Canada, donating over 10,000 end-of-day meals to shelters, winning federal grants to help organizations upgrade their accessibility, and organizing COVID-19 relief projects with over $5,000 in funding.
